My Nigerian Jollof Rice

Nigerian jollof rice is a staple in west africa cuisine .it is made from rice,tomatoes,onion,peppers and other seasonings.it is a rich and aromatic,tasty west african one-pot meal.it is similar to jambalaya but with distinctive african spices.

1

Details about Nigerian jollof rice

Preparation time:30 mins

Cook time:1 hour

Servings:8 people

Difficulty:intermediate

Ingredients

2
  1. 8 cups of parboiled long grain rice
  2. 1 bowl of fresh ripe tomatoes either crushed or blended
  3. 6 cups of water
  4. 3 cups of olive oil
  5. 4 tumeric teaspoon
  6. 1 teaspoon of currypowder
  7. 1 bayleaf

instructions

3
  1. Combine tomato,red pepper,ginger and water in a blender for the sauce,blend on high until smooth and set aside.
  2. Heat olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat then add onion and salt umtil onion starts to soften in 4 to f minutes.clear space in the middle of the pan and add tomato for 2 minutes,season with curry powder,pepper and turmeric,continue for two minutes until everything is well combined.
  3. Turn off the heat,stir in the rice,making sure every grain of rice is coated with the oil-tomato mixture then add bayleaf and chicken boullion paste
  4. Turn heat to high and stir in tomato sauce and bring to a vigorous simmer.cover with a tight fitting lid,reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for 20 minutes,do not remove the lid or stir rice.
  5. Turn off heat after 20 minutes,and set the timer for 12 minutes.allow rice to sit and do not remove the lid.
  6. After the 12 minutes are up remove lid and fluff rice with a fork,seperating all the grains.serve garnished with green onions and chopped cilantro.
  7. If rice seems to be a little undercooked at this point ,pat the rice gently with the back of the fork,cover tightly again and set heat to medium low.cook for another 5 minutes then your food is ready.

    8. NOTE:Nigerian jollof rice is best enjoyed while hot in order to feel the tantalizing and pepper taste and it is best served with chilled water.
